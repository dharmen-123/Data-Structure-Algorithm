    // //  ***** OPERATOR *****  // //

    An operator is a symbol that tells the compiler to perform a specific operation.
    int sum = a + b;
    Here + is an operator, a and b are operands.
 
 // // ** BODMAS Rule ** // //
 B - Bracket 
 O - Open 
 D - Divisible
 M - Multiply 
 A - Addition 
 S - Subtraction

 Example - 
 2-8/4*10+6 = -12

    ******* Types of Operator *******

1. Arithmetic Operators
Used for mathematical calculations
 Two types of Arithmetic operator
 
(a) Unary
    Increment & Decrement Operators
    Used to increase or decrease value by 1
    🔹 Operators:
    ++ , --

    🔹 Types:
    Pre-increment: ++a
    Post-increment: a++
  
(b) Binary
   🔹 Operators:
    + , - , * , / , %

2. Relational (Comparison) Operators
Used to compare two values → returns true or false

🔹 Operators:
== , != , > , < , >= , <=

3. Logical Operators
Used to combine conditions

🔹 Operators:
&& (AND), || (OR), ! (NOT)

4. Assignment Operators
Used to assign values to variables

🔹 Operators:
= , += , -= , *= , /= , %=

5. Bitwise Operators

Operate on binary representation (bits)

🔹 Operators:

& , | , ^ , ~ , << , >>

* Conditional (Ternary) Operator
Shortcut for if-else

🔹 Syntax:
condition ? expression1 : expression2;
